Project Overview

I approached the redesign of Energy.gov in way that could maintain the look and feel of the organizations mission, yet build upon the design to freshen the aesthetic and improve the information architecture. I pulled in plenty of user research to navigate the improvements needed to create a greater overall experience for visitors of Energy.gov

The Problem

Users found it difficult to navigate through the information architecture presented across energy.gov. Image placement and size created an odd hierarchy that made it confusing for users to successfully consume the information they were looking for.

The Solution

Update the navigation architecture throughout energy.gov to provide users with an easier experience finding relevant information while improving the overall look and feel.

Research

User Paths

I defined 3 differed user paths during testing to examine how easy or hard it was for users to locate certain information within energy.gov.

Navigate through the website to find the energy programs related to the state you live in

Find information about home lighting choices that can help save you money

Find an article about the energy department's inventions saving you energy & money at home

User Testing Observations

General dislike about how many pages were needed to be clicked through to find necessary information

A majority of users did not understand what the purpose of energy.gov

Users felt that energy.gov was just a collection of randomly scattered articles
